## Binary To Hexadecimal 
Here conversion of binary to number

### For example
we have an binary number

1] *01001110111.110101010* (base 2)

where base '2' indicates Binary

The above binary number make a group of four 

*0100,1111,0111,1101,0101,0000* (base 2) - Binary

Binary to Hexadecimal

*4.   F.   7.   D.   5.   0* (base 16) - Hexadecimal

The final value gives after conversion from binary to hexadecimal

--------------------------
| 4 F 7 . D 5 0 (base 16) |   Hexadecimal
--------------------------

*010011110111.110101010 (base 2) = 4F7.D50 (base 16)*

## Hxadecimal To Binary
The conversion of hexadecimal to binary

### For Example
we have an Hexadecimal number

1] *F37A.B2* (base 16)
 
 where base '16' indicates Hexadecimal

 Here each Hexadecimal digits consider an four binary bits 

 where:-
 
*F37A.B2* (base 16)

F = 1111 , 3 = 0011 , A = 1010 . B = 1011 , 2 = 0010
 
Hexadecimal to Binary

*1111,0011,1010 . 1011,0010* (base 2) Binary

The final value gives after conversion from Hexadecimal to Binary

-----------------------------
| 1111,0011,1010 . 1011,0010 |
-----------------------------

*F37A.B2* =  *1111,0011,1010 . 1011,0010*

## successive Division Radix Conversion
Successive division (or radix division) is a method for converting an integer from a source base (usually decimal) to a target base by repeatedly dividing the quotient by the new radix and recording the remainders. 

 ## For Example

1] *119* (base 10) convert itnto binary

now solving problem 

1. Divide 119 by 2
  119 / 2 = 59       1

2. Divide 59 by 2
   59 / 2 = 29       1
   
3. Divide 29 by 2
    29 / 2 = 14      1
4. Divide 14 by 2
    14 / 2 = 7       0
