## Register Basics 
A register are high speed temporary storage devices located within the cpu, composed of group of flip-flops that stores multiple bits (N flip-flops for N-buit data).

- Structure : A register is a collection of flip-flops; an n-bit register uses n flip-flops to store an n-bit word. 

- Function: They provide raoid temporary storage for intermediate results,memory address, or instructions currently begin executed

- Shift Register Types
  - SISO (Serial in Serial Out)
  - SIPO (Serial in Parallel Out)
  - PISO (Parallel in Serial Out)
  - PIPO (Parallel in Parallel Out)
